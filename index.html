Good Times with Bad Movies

A Love Letter to Truly Awful Movies

Movies are a universal form of entertainment that unite people across generations. Whether they’re cinematic masterpieces or spectacularly terrible, every film has its own distinctive charm. Here, we pay tribute to the latter—those flawed gems we love to hate (and secretly love to love).

On Bad Movies

Truly bad movies have a remarkable ability to bring audiences together in laughter, disbelief, and occasional awe. Perhaps the plot twists make no sense, or the dialogue feels hilariously out of place. Yet there’s something strangely endearing about a film that’s so far off the mark that it circles back around to become oddly captivating.

My System for Ranking Movies

While many critics evaluate films by their technical merits—such as acting, screenplay, or visual effects—I take a different approach. My system rewards creativity (whether intended or accidental), celebrates over‐the‐top performances, and values pure entertainment above all else. No matter how many glaring issues a movie might have, if it captivates me for all the wrong (or right) reasons, it deserves a spot on this list.
